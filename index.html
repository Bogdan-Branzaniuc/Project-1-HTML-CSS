Fusion

Fusion is achieved by heating and maintaingin a plasma at extreme temperatures and density, where the atoms are loosing their electrons and start coliding with each other creating a heavier element.

As a result of this process, energy is released up to 4 milion times more than burning the same quantity of fossil fuels.

The best example of Fusion Reaction is our Sun.

Due to it’s mass and gravity, the necessary density and temperature allows for Nuclear Fusion to occur.

Fision

In a Fission reaction, we are looking at a bigger atom that gets split into two or more smaller nuclei by a proton traveling at an imense speed.

This process generates further high velocity protons, creating a chain reaction that needs to be maintained.

Splitting the atom generates an incredible amount of energy.

Unlike Fusion, Fission requires a bigger responsibility and risk, more safety measures, and nculear waste gets created

Fusion efficency

On 21 December 2021 Joint European Tours (JET) had an output of 59 megajoules. The fusion reactor produced around 11 Megawatts over 5 seconds.

If this energy would have been harvested, the total stored energy would be around 4MW

However In total, when JET runs, it consumes 700 to 800 MW of electrical power (the equivalent of 1-2% of the UK's total electricity usage).

This is to say that there’s still a long way to go,
